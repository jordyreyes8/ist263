Injury Recovery

Foot and Ankle Injury Recovery

The RICE treatment is a popular method of recovering from minor to moderate foot and ankle injuries. RICE stands for rest, ice, compression, and elevation. You should rest the injured foot/ankle, ice it, compress it with elastic bandages and elevate it on something like a pillow.

Hip and Thigh Injury Recovery

The RICE treatment is also a good treatment for hip and thigh injuries. A crucial part about recovering from these injuries is resting for enough time. It can take up to 6 weeks or more to heal from a hip strain and a thigh strain can take 10 days to 3 weeks to fully heal.

Knee Injury Recovery

To recover from minor to moderate knee injuries it is important that you take a break from physical activity and rest your knee as much as possible. You should also apply ice packs frequently to reduce pain and swelling and follow the RICE treatment.

Wrist and Hand Injury Recovery

To recover from wrist and hand injuries you should refrain from physical activity and use the RICE treatment for at least a week. For more serious injuries a doctor may recommend you put your hand in a cast for a few weeks to a few months.

Head and Face Injury Recovery

If you suffered from a head injury you should immediately take some time out to relax and recover. If the injury is more serious you should visit a doctor immediately.
